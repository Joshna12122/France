Religion plays a complex role in French culture. France is known for its commitment to secularism, which means that the country promotes the separation of Church and State and ensures the neutrality of public institutions. This strong emphasis on secularism is rooted in the principle of laïcité. In France, individuals have the freedom to practice any religion of their choice. The government guarantees freedom of religion and protects the rights of religious minorities. However, the state remains neutral in religious matters and does not favor any particular religion
